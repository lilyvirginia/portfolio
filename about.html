What do I do?

I am a UX designer dedicated to strategic solutions based on in-depth user research. My strengths lie in my communication and analytical skills.

I especially enjoy finding creative and holistic approaches to complex problems.

Who am I?

You’re reading this because hopefully you’re wondering if we should work together. Perhaps you’re thinking ’is Lily a good fit for my company? What skills does she bring to the table? Can I trust her?’

While I’m about to tell you all the ways I’m awesome and interesting, my greatest strength lies in my willingness to be vulnerable. As an artist and human being, I’ve always been fascinated with creating honest connection. Naturally UX design is a perfect fit... I am pretty much a walking ball of empathy.

I like to bring that honest approach to my research as well as my day to day interactions.

How did I get here?

When I was 24, I took a cross-country roadtrip from California to the East Coast. I landed in Brooklyn with a job in music for film. I worked there for several years as the sole employee, learning a lot about music production but also how to manage several simultaneous projects, wear a million different hats and, essentially, run a small company.

Songwriting had always been a part of my life and there came a time where I knew I had to take the leap, quit my job, and figure out how to live courageously as an independent artist.

To make ends meet, I started my own company in children’s entertainment. Both of these experiences, running my children’s music company and recording, promoting, and releasing records under my own name solidified my knowledge of social media marketing, digital strategy, overall professionalism and most importantly, exploring connection.

I started to wonder ‘how can I better explore connection in our digital lives?’ This led me to take part in several hackathons for my music projects, go to tech lectures for fun, and eventually I started to learn more about UX design. For me, it was the perfect combination of techical skills, analytical smarts and a compassionate approach to making our technological lives more human. I was hooked.

So I decided to dive in head first and enrolled in a year-long UX design program, all the while continuing to run my company.

I’ve since had the opportunity to work on several fascinating contract projects and am currently looking to join a great company where I can contribute my skills, solve exciting challenges, and grow along the way.

Lily
