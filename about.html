What do I do?

I am a UX designer dedicated to strategic solutions based on in-depth user research. My strengths lie in my communication and analytical skills.

I enjoy finding creative approaches to complex problems.

How do I do it?

  • Grit

    As a small business owner for the past 4 years, I know what it takes to get a new idea off the ground and running... grit and the willingness to roll up your sleeves and work hard.

  • Focus

    As an independent artist in NYC for 8 years, I've always had to keep myself motivated and focused to achieve my goals. For instance:

    • Single-handedly raised 9K on Kickstarter with 163 backers.
    • Produced and released 3 albums.
    • Built a mailing list of 2k.
    • Maintained a monthly newsletter.
    • Managed & Strategized all social media.
    • Performed over 1,000 shows
  • Curiosity

    I am fascinated by the world around me and always want to learn more. I bring that curious energy to everything I do.

  • OCD

    Don't let my sunny disposition fool you, I am a secret perfectionist. From my audio-editing experience working on films for National Geographic etc. I know that being half-a-second off in your edit can mean a day's worth of work. I work hard to respect my colleagues' time by presenting great deliverables and paying attention to the details, so others don't have to pick up my slack.

  • 'What if?'

    Whether it's writing a song, or thinking up better solutions to an app, the beauty of creativity is that it allows you to imagine something that doesn't exist yet. I allow myself to ask 'what if' to easily come up with new ideas.

  • Empathy

    My greatest strength lies in my willingness to be vulnerable. I believe it helps me understand others better and bring true empathy to the work that I do in UX Design and beyond.

Why do I do it?

I’ve always been fascinated with creating honest connection and exploring the human condition within technology. As an artist I explored that theme through many interactive multi-media projects every time asking myself - 'how can I bring more humanity to this digital experience?'

About a year ago, I realized that I had unofficially doing UX for years and it was about time I made it official! I joined a year-long bootcamp to finesse my skills

How did I get here?

When I was 24, I took a cross-country roadtrip from California to the East Coast. I landed in Brooklyn with a job in music for film. I worked there for several years as the sole employee, learning a lot about music production but also how to manage several simultaneous projects, wear a million different hats and, essentially, run a small company.

Songwriting had always been a part of my life and there came a time where I knew I had to take the leap, quit my job, and figure out how to live courageously as an independent artist.

To make ends meet, I started my own company in children’s entertainment. Both of these experiences, running my children’s music company and recording, promoting, and releasing records under my own name solidified my knowledge of social media marketing, digital strategy, overall professionalism and most importantly, exploring connection.

I started to wonder ‘how can I better explore connection in our digital lives?’ This led me to take part in several hackathons for my music projects, go to tech lectures for fun, and eventually I started to learn more about UX design. For me, it was the perfect combination of techical skills, analytical smarts and a compassionate approach to making our technological lives more human. I was hooked.

So I decided to dive in head first and enrolled in a year-long UX design program, all the while continuing to run my company.

I’ve since had the opportunity to work on several fascinating contract projects and am currently looking to join a great company where I can contribute my skills, solve exciting challenges, and grow along the way.
